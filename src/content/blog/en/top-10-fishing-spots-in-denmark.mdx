Denmark has no mountains, no big lakes by Nordic standards and a highest point of 170.86 m — and yet it might be Europe's most complete fishing destination. The reason is the coast: 8,750 km of fjords, points and shallow bays where sea trout feed within casting range of shore, plus restored Jutland rivers carrying their own strain of Atlantic salmon and a put-and-take network so dense you can fish a stocked lake within twenty minutes of almost anywhere. One inexpensive national licence, the fisketegn, opens most of it.

## The coast of Funen: sea trout as a way of life

Fyn (Funen) markets itself as the sea trout island, and it has earned it: more than 1,100 km of varied coastline, decades of stream restoration and smolt stocking, and a mapped network of over 100 marked fishing spots. Classic marks like Helnæs, Æbelø and the Svendborg Sound points fish all year — spring and autumn for numbers, winter for the biggest silver fish on milder days. Wading anglers cast spoons, inline lures and shrimp flies over leopard-spotted sand and bladderwrack. Knold & Tot, the island's famous guiding culture and the Havørred Fyn project make it the easiest place in Europe to learn coastal sea trout fishing.

## Skjern Å: Denmark's own salmon river

West Jutland's Skjern River, rebuilt in the 2000s in Denmark's largest nature restoration project, now produces the country's biggest Atlantic salmon — fish over 20 lb every season, descendants of an original Danish strain saved at the last moment. The season opens 16 April with a quota system; the lower meadows around Borris and Sønder Felding are prime beats. Nearby, the Varde Å and Ribe Vesterå carry growing runs of their own. A salmon from these slow, peat-stained rivers, fought among grazing cattle, is one of angling's quieter triumphs.

## Karup Å: the sea trout river of records

The Karup, winding through central Jutland to the Limfjord, is spoken of in the same breath as the world's great sea trout rivers. Its deep, undercut bends hold sea trout averaging far heavier than the coast — fish of 5 to 8 kg surprise nobody, and the river's record fish are into double figures. Night fishing with big wet flies or quietly swung spinners from June to September is the classic approach. Local associations around Karup and Hagebro sell day cards; respect the meadows and the famously protective riparian customs.

## The Limfjord and Mors: island fishing in an inland sea

The Limfjord slices Jutland in two, and its central islands — Mors above all — offer sheltered sea trout water in every wind, which is why coastal regulars love it. Points like Hanklit and Sallingsund fish well from March to November, and the fjord's revived oyster and mussel beds tell of improving water quality. Jesperhus and the Legind Sø area add family options, while autumn brings garfish runs and the chance of a fat fjord herring session from the harbours of Nykøbing Mors.

## Bornholm: spring sea trout in the Baltic

The granite island of Bornholm, far out in the Baltic, runs on a different calendar: February to May is high season, when sea trout cruise the rocky points at Salene and Hammeren — and the island has produced fish over 10 kg. The deep, clear water close to shore means big fish patrol within range of a long cast. Combine it with cod jigging from Tejn or Nexø and the island's smoked-herring smokehouses, and you have the Baltic's most charismatic short fishing trip.

## Copenhagen Harbour and the Øresund

Denmark's capital is a serious fishery. The cleaned-up harbour — championed by the Københavns Havns Sportsfisker Laug, the harbour's own angling guild — produces sea trout, garfish, herring and even flatfish along its quays. Minutes away, the Øresund strait, protected from trawling since 1932, holds some of Europe's best cod fishing, with charter boats out of Helsingør and Vedbæk jigging fish year-round, plus spring plaice and autumn herring. Few capitals anywhere let you catch a sea trout on your lunch break.

## The Gudenå: Denmark's longest river

The 160 km Gudenå, from its springs near Tørring to Randers Fjord, packs in grayling and brown trout in the upper reaches, big sea trout below the Tange dam, and pike and zander through the lake chain around Silkeborg, where the river threads Denmark's lake highlands. The Tangeværket stretch is famous for heavyweight sea trout in autumn; the Silkeborg lakes respond well to trolling and modern perch tactics. Canoe traffic is heavy in July — fish early, late or in the shoulder seasons.

## Put-and-take nation: stocked lakes everywhere

Denmark invented the modern European put-and-take format, and western Jutland is its heartland: waters like Vestjyllands Put and Take, the Søndervig Put & Take lake behind the North Sea dunes, Hvilested Lystfiskersøer near Kolding and the holiday-island favourite Rømø Fiskesø offer stocked rainbow trout — frequently 2 to 5 kg — on simple hourly tickets with no licence required. Quality varies, so look for clear water, aerators and frequent stocking notices. For families, rusty casters and travellers with two spare hours, the format is unbeatable.

## The Vejle and Kolding fjord streams

East Jutland's short, lively streams — the Vejle Å, Kolding Å and Grejs Å — drain beech-forested valleys into calm fjords and carry strong sea trout runs for their size. The fjord mouths themselves fish superbly in winter when colder coasts go quiet, with the inner Vejle Fjord a known big-fish bet in February. Day cards are cheap, access paths are well marked, and the whole package sits an hour from Billund airport, making it many visitors' first Danish cast.

## Langeland: flatfish, cod and trophy coast

The long, thin island of Langeland is Denmark's saltwater all-rounder: legendary boat fishing over the Langeland Belt's deep edges for cod and the spring run of big migrating sea trout along Påø and Ristinge beaches. Summer brings superb flatfish from boat and shore — dab, plaice and flounder — and autumn sees garfish and herring stack into Bagenkop harbour. Rental boats from Spodsbjerg are an institution; drifting the belt with shad-tipped pirks remains the classic method.
