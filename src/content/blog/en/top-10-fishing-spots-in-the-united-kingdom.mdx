There are roughly 200 chalk streams on the planet, and England holds about 85 percent of them — rivers filtered through chalk aquifers until they run gin-clear over bright gravel. That accident of geology gave the world modern dry-fly fishing, but it is only one chapter of British angling. Scotland's salmon rivers invented the double-handed cast that bears the Spey's name, Cornwall's kelp-fringed coast has become Europe's wrasse laboratory, and the Midlands and south-east are dense with commercial stillwaters where carp fishing was practically reinvented. A single rod licence covers England and Wales; Scotland works on permits per beat. Here are the ten places that matter.

## 1–2. The Test and the Itchen, Hampshire

The Test and its quieter neighbour the Itchen are the most famous trout streams on earth — spring-fed, weed-waving, alive with wild browns and grayling. Fishing is almost entirely by named beat through estates and clubs around Stockbridge and Winchester, with day rods running from reasonable to eye-watering on hallowed water like the Houghton Club's. Mayfly time in late May and early June — "duffers' fortnight" — is the easiest fishing of the season; the technical sport comes later, on pale wateries and tiny spurwings. Book months ahead.

## 3. The River Spey, Speyside

Fast, broad and amber-tinted, the Spey gave its name to the casting style its tree-lined banks demanded. The salmon season runs 11 February to 30 September, with spring fish in the upper beats and bigger summer runs through Grantown-on-Spey, where the local angling association offers visitors some of Scotland's best-value salmon and sea-trout water — roughly 12 km of double-bank fishing. Sea-trout at dusk in June is the connoisseur's game. Whisky distilleries line the valley, which helps morale on blank days.

## 4. The River Tweed, Scottish Borders

The Tweed is Britain's most prolific salmon river, with a season stretching from 1 February to 30 November and a famous autumn run that fills beats around Kelso and Coldstream — the Junction Pool, where the Teviot meets the Tweed, is arguably the most celebrated salmon pool anywhere. No Sunday salmon fishing, by long Scottish custom. Beats book through agencies, and the middle river offers genuinely affordable days outside peak autumn weeks. Brown trout and grayling fishing on association tickets is an underrated bonus.

## 5. The Cornish wrasse coast

Cornwall's granite headlands — the Lizard, Penwith, the marks around Newquay and Falmouth — have become Europe's proving ground for hard rock fishing: targeting ballan wrasse on soft plastics over kelp and boulder ground. Ballans past 50 cm fight like underwater bulldogs, and the catch-and-release ethic is near universal because wrasse grow slowly and hold tiny territories. Add summer bass on surface lures, mackerel from every pier and the chance of a porbeagle offshore, and the far south-west earns its place on any list.

## 6. Chew Valley Lake, Somerset

This 1,200-acre reservoir south of Bristol is England's most famous big-fish stillwater twice over: a top-tier rainbow and brown trout fishery, and the venue whose pike trials produce fish over 40 lb often enough to bend national record talk every winter. Fly fishing for pike was largely popularised here. Day tickets and boats book through Bristol Water, and the limited pike days sell out within minutes of release — set a calendar reminder.

## 7. The Norfolk Broads

The Broads' 200 km of navigable rivers and shallow lagoons make up Britain's best big-pike water outside Scotland, with the Thurne system — Hickling Broad, Horsey Mere — historically producing thirty-pounders over its prolific baitfish shoals. Summer brings superb bream and rudd fishing from free or cheap bank stretches, all of it among windmills and reed beds. A boat transforms the place; electric outboards are welcome and several yards hire fishing dinghies by the day.

## 8. The River Wye, Welsh borders

The Wye winds 250 km from Plynlimon to the Severn estuary and is England and Wales' premier barbel river, with double-figure fish from the runs around Hereford and Ross-on-Wye, plus chub, pike and a recovering salmon stock. The Wye and Usk Foundation's passport scheme opens dozens of farm and estate beats to day-ticket anglers — a model of access. High summer evenings, feeder rods bouncing in the streamy water, are as good as coarse river fishing gets in Britain.

## 9–10. The southern stillwater circuit

The UK perfected the commercial fishery, and two stand-ins for the whole genre belong here. Marsh Farm near Godalming, Surrey, is the country's shrine to the crucian carp — true crucians to specimen size in purpose-built ponds, fished with lift-method floats at dawn. In the south-east, complexes such as Wylands International Angling Centre in East Sussex and Catch 22 Fishing Centre in Norfolk offer carp into the twenties of pounds, full facilities and night fishing — the easiest possible entry point for newcomers or travelling anglers with an evening to spare.
