A decade ago, planning a fishing session meant checking a printed tide table, calling a local tackle shop for conditions, and hoping the weather app on your phone was accurate to within a couple of hours. Today the calculation is different. Purpose-built fishing apps pull real-time atmospheric pressure data, satellite-based sea-surface temperature maps, solunar peak tables, and coastal tide predictions directly to your pocket. The technology has matured considerably, and separating the genuinely useful apps from the overcrowded, advertisement-bloated alternatives is worth doing methodically.

## Tide and Current Apps

Tides matter more than many freshwater anglers realise, and for coastal and estuarine fishing they are often the single most important variable. Tides Near Me (iOS/Android) offers clear harmonic tide predictions for over 10,000 stations worldwide, covering the UK, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, and North American coasts in impressive depth. Tidegraph Pro provides layered data including current velocity, depth at wharves, and moon phase alongside the tide curve — useful for rock fishing and inshore boat fishing where current speed determines feeding windows. In the UK, PlanetSea Tide Charts uses real UK Hydrographic Office data rather than harmonic modelling, which gives it an accuracy edge in complex tidal channels like the Bristol Channel and The Solent. For US anglers, NOAA Tides and Currents (free, web and app) remains the gold standard for accuracy at over 3,000 official monitoring stations.

## Solunar Calendars and Feeding Activity

The solunar theory — developed by John Alden Knight in 1926 — proposes that fish and wildlife feeding activity peaks during specific periods related to the sun and moon positions. While empirical evidence is mixed, many experienced anglers find a real correlation between solunar major and minor periods and fishing activity, particularly for species like bass, walleye, snapper, and trevally that respond strongly to tidal and light cues. Fishbrain (iOS/Android) includes a solunar calendar integrated with catch-log data from its large user community, allowing you to see what species were caught during what solunar periods in your region. Mossy Oak Fishing also incorporates a well-designed solunar table. The free app Fishing Points (iOS/Android) combines tide, solunar, and wind data in a clean interface and is particularly popular in the UK and Europe.

## Weather and Wind Forecasting

Wind direction and speed determine casting comfort, surface conditions, and in many situations, fish feeding behaviour — offshore, a steady wind pushes baitfish to the lee shore and concentrates predators behind it. Windy (iOS/Android, free) is the favourite of most experienced marine anglers worldwide: it pulls from multiple global weather models (GFS, ECMWF, NAM) and displays animated wind and wave layers on a map in real time. The wave-height overlay is particularly valuable for offshore anglers deciding whether to launch. Windguru, originally a windsurfing app, has become a staple for sea anglers throughout Europe and South Africa because of its high-resolution coastal-grid forecasting at up to 9-day range. For freshwater anglers, Weather Underground offers hyper-local readings from its network of privately operated weather stations, often giving a more accurate picture than national weather services in complex inland terrain.

## Catch Logging and Social Apps

Keeping a fishing diary is one of the most productive things an angler can do. Patterns emerge across seasons — the same pool fishing well in the same week of October for three consecutive years; the same morning tide producing bass in June regardless of year. Fishbrain is the market leader for social catch logging, with over 15 million registered users who have collectively logged tens of millions of catches with species, location, depth, lure, and conditions data. The data density makes its heatmap feature — showing where species have been recently reported — genuinely useful, though its free tier limits depth of access. Anglr (US-focused, iOS/Android) is preferred by many serious freshwater anglers for its integration with GPS track recording — it creates a route log of your fishing session that you can overlay on underwater contour maps. Fishidy, another social fishing app, is particularly strong on the Great Lakes and Mississippi river system.

## Fish Identification Apps

Correctly identifying a catch matters for legal compliance, conservation, and informed release. iNaturalist (iOS/Android, free) uses community verification and machine learning to identify fish from photographs, and its database extends to tens of thousands of species worldwide. It also contributes sighting data to global biodiversity records. Seek (from iNaturalist) uses on-device image recognition and is useful when you lack mobile data. The UK's Centre for Environment, Fisheries and Aquaculture Science (CEFAS) offers a dedicated UK sea fish identification guide via its FishID tool, which is authoritative for British coastal species. In Australia, the Fisheries Research and Development Corporation's FISH-ID app covers over 300 species found in Australian waters and is built to help anglers determine whether a fish meets minimum legal size requirements in each state.

## Mapping and Bathymetry Apps

Seeing underwater structure — channels, drop-offs, submerged points, weed-bed edges — transforms location selection from guesswork to strategy. Navionics (iOS/Android) is the global standard for marine and lake chart plotting, covering coastal waters, lakes, and rivers across North America, Europe, Australia, and beyond. Its Sonar Chart layer, built from crowdsourced sonar readings contributed by users, provides detail on freshwater lakes that would otherwise have no commercial chart. Deeper Sonar, which pairs a castable sonar device with an app, creates real-time bathymetric maps of any water you cast it into — valuable for unfamiliar reservoirs and gravel pits. C-MAP (iOS/Android) is preferred by many professional charter skippers for its regularly updated offshore charts and 3D seabed view.

## Regulations and Licensing Apps

Knowing bag limits, season dates, and minimum sizes is a legal requirement and an ethical one. In the UK, the Environment Agency's Fishing Licence app allows you to purchase a rod licence directly and confirms what is valid for a given water type. In the US, iSportsman and iAngler Tournament include state-by-state fishing regulations integrated with the catch-log function. In Australia, the NSW DPI RecFish database and the Queensland RuleBase app both let you look up species-specific regulations by postcode or map location in seconds.
