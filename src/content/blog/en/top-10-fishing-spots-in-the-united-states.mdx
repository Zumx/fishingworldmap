The United States compresses almost every kind of fishing on the planet into a single country. You can swing flies for wild king salmon in Alaska in June, sight-cast to tailing bonefish in the Florida Keys in November, and troll for walleye on Lake Erie in between — all on the same passport, often on the same trip. What follows is a ten-stop tour of the fisheries American anglers themselves dream about, arranged roughly from north to south, with honest notes on timing, access and the licences you will need along the way.

## 1. The Kenai River, Alaska

Two hours south of Anchorage, the Kenai is the most famous salmon river in North America and the water that produced the 44 kg world-record king salmon in 1985. The late run of kings enters in July, sockeye flood the river in their hundreds of thousands from mid-July, and enormous rainbow trout gorge on eggs behind them into October. The middle river around Cooper Landing and the Russian River confluence is the classic drift-boat stretch. Book guides a year ahead for peak weeks, and check the Alaska Department of Fish and Game emergency orders before you travel — king regulations change weekly.

## 2. Bristol Bay and the Naknek, Alaska

Farther west, reachable only by floatplane, Bristol Bay hosts the largest sockeye salmon run on Earth — more than 60 million fish returned in the record year of 2022. Lodges on the Naknek, Kvichak and Alagnak rivers offer rainbow trout that average over 50 cm, Arctic char, grayling and five salmon species. The trade-off is cost and a short season: mid-June to late September, with the famous mouse-pattern rainbow fishing peaking in August.

## 3. The Bighorn River, Montana

Below Yellowtail Dam at Fort Smith, the Bighorn is a tailwater conveyor belt of brown and rainbow trout, with fish counts that have historically exceeded 4,000 trout per mile in the upper 13 miles. Flows stay cold and fishable nearly year-round; April baetis hatches and the August black caddis are the marquee events. Access is straightforward through three public ramps, and Montana's nonresident licence can be bought online in minutes.

## 4. The White River, Arkansas

The Ozark tailwaters below Bull Shoals and Norfork dams hold staggering numbers of stocked rainbows and a genuine chance at a brown trout over ten pounds — the river produced a former world record of 18.25 kg. Winter is prime time for the biggest browns, when minimum flows let anglers wade gravel bars around Cotter and Rim Shoals. Resort culture here is delightfully old-school: flat-bottomed jon boats, shore lunches, and trout fishing 365 days a year.

## 5. Lake Erie's Western Basin, Ohio

Erie is called the Walleye Capital of the World for good reason: recent hatches have pushed the population into the tens of millions. From April through June, drifting weight-forward spinners around the Bass Islands and Port Clinton produces limits of golden walleye, while summer trolling spreads catch fish suspended over deeper water. Smallmouth bass fishing around Kelleys Island is world-class in its own right. Charter fleets out of Port Clinton and Sandusky make this one of the most accessible big-water fisheries in the country.

## 6. Montauk, New York

Every autumn the entire Atlantic striped bass migration funnels past Montauk Point at the tip of Long Island. From September into November, blitzes of stripers, bluefish and false albacore erupt under birds within casting range of the famous lighthouse rocks. Surfcasters fish around the clock; light-tackle boats run from Montauk Harbor. Striped bass are managed with a tight slot limit — currently one fish between 71 and 79 cm in New York waters — so check the season's rules before keeping anything.

## 7. The Outer Banks, North Carolina

Cape Hatteras juts so far into the Atlantic that the Gulf Stream passes within about 20 km of the beach, which is why Oregon Inlet's charter fleet can reach blue marlin, yellowfin tuna and mahi-mahi on day trips. Surf anglers chase red drum on the points in October and November, and the long village piers at Avalon, Nags Head and Rodanthe give visitors easy access to Spanish mackerel and pompano. A single North Carolina coastal licence covers it all.

## 8. Venice, Louisiana

Where the Mississippi River shatters into its delta, nutrient-rich water builds the most fertile inshore fishery in America. Venice marsh fishing means bull redfish over 10 kg sight-cast in knee-deep water, plus speckled trout in numbers that feel unfair. Offshore, the same port is one of the world's premier yellowfin tuna fisheries, with fish caught year-round around the deep-water oil platforms. October through March brings the calmest marsh conditions and the biggest reds.

## 9. Florida's Gulf Coast piers and flats

Florida deserves two entries, and the Gulf side comes first. Around Sarasota and Charlotte Harbor, snook stack up in passes such as those near Lemon Bay each summer, while tarpon over 45 kg pour along the beaches of Boca Grande in May and June. Visitors without a boat are spoiled: the Dunedin Municipal Pier, the long pier at Dania Beach on the Atlantic side, and managed waters like the Tenoroc Fish Management Area near Lakeland offer genuinely good fishing for the price of a parking spot. Freshwater fans can add Lake Okeechobee, arguably the best trophy largemouth bass lake in the country, with the bite peaking December through March.

## 10. The Florida Keys

The chain of islands from Key Largo to Key West is the spiritual home of flats fishing. Islamorada calls itself the Sport Fishing Capital of the World, and the surrounding flats hold bonefish, permit and the giant migratory tarpon that arrive each spring — the prime window runs March to June. Offshore, sailfish peak in winter on the Atlantic edge. Skiff guides are essential for the flats and book out months in advance, but bridge fishing along the Overseas Highway costs nothing and produces tarpon, snapper and sharks for patient anglers.

## Planning, licences and timing

Every state sells its own licence, almost all of them online, and short-term visitor permits typically cost between 15 and 40 dollars. Saltwater and freshwater licences are often separate, and Alaska adds a king salmon stamp. The broad seasonal logic: Alaska and the northern trout rivers fish best June to September, the Great Lakes peak in spring, and the entire southern saltwater belt — Louisiana, the Gulf Coast, the Keys — is at its best from autumn through spring when northern waters are frozen.
