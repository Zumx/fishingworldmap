Belarus rarely makes fishing bucket lists, and that is precisely its appeal. A flat, glacier-carved country of 20,000 rivers and around 11,000 lakes, it hides some of Eastern Europe's healthiest stocks of pike, zander, perch and wels catfish in waters that see a fraction of the pressure found further west. The Braslav Lakes glitter in the north, the Pripyat winds through Europe's largest intact marshland in the south, and in between lie reservoirs, oxbows and fish farms where a day's fishing still costs less than a city lunch. Here are the ten waters to know.

## The Braslav Lakes — pike water of national-park rank

In the far northwest, the Braslav Lakes National Park gathers roughly 300 lakes, from the 36 km² Drivyaty down to weedy pots between eskers. Snudy and Strusto are famous for big pike that follow whitefish schools, while Drivyaty's reed edges hold zander and coarse fish in numbers. Ice fishing here is a December-to-March institution, and in open water trolling the drop-offs at 4-8 m is the local method for the crocodile-class fish. The town of Braslav has boat hire, guides and fishing permits for the park's waters.

## The Pripyat River — marshland catfish and floodplain pike

The Pripyat drains the Polesia marshes, a wetland system so vast it is nicknamed the Amazon of Europe. Around Turov and within reach of Pripyatsky National Park, the river's deep outside bends hold wels catfish that genuinely exceed 50 kg, taken on clonking gear and big deadbaits at dusk. In spring the floodplain becomes a maze of warm shallows where pike spawn and ide feed; by summer the oxbows fish like jungle lagoons. Birdlife — terns, storks, white-tailed eagles — is constant company.

## Lake Naroch — the country's inland sea

Naroch is the largest lake in Belarus at nearly 80 km², ringed by sanatoriums and pine forest. Its clear, hard-bottomed water grows quality zander and big perch, and whitefish (selyava) support a traditional fishery. The lake is part of a protected zone, so check the current rules — certain areas and seasons are closed — but the adjacent lakes Myastro and Batorino offer easier access for pike and bream sessions from a rowing boat.

## The Vileyka Reservoir — zander factory near Minsk

The largest artificial water in the country, the Vileyka Reservoir feeds Minsk's water system and holds a dense zander population along its flooded river channel. Vertical jigging over the old Viliya riverbed in autumn produces fish of 2-4 kg with pleasing regularity, plus night-bite sessions in summer. Being 80 km from the capital, it absorbs weekend pressure yet keeps producing — testament to strong natural recruitment.

## Zaslavl Reservoir — the Minsk Sea

Twenty minutes from the capital, the so-called Minsk Sea is where city anglers cut their teeth: bream and roach for the float crowd, perch on drop-shot along the dam riprap, pike around the islands in October. Infrastructure is excellent — rental boats, promenades, winter ice villages of tip-up anglers. It is not wilderness, but as an after-work fishery for a capital city it is remarkably good.

## The Berezina — slow water through a biosphere reserve

The Berezina, famous from Napoleonic history, runs partly through the Berezinsky Biosphere Reserve, where beavers, otters and even bears share the banks. Its unhurried current, fallen timber and deep clay holes suit big ide, chub, pike and the occasional river zander. Canoe-and-fish floats of two or three days, camping on sandy banks, are the way locals do it — and outfitters in Borisov can arrange the same for visitors.

## The Neman headwaters — stream fishing in the west

Rising southwest of Minsk and flowing toward Grodno, the upper Neman and tributaries like the Shchara offer something different: faster water with chub, asp and dace, plus barbel in the deeper runs. Around Grodno, the river broadens beneath the old castle and the Augustow Canal adds lock-pool fishing in a UNESCO-tentative landscape. Light spinning with small crankbaits in summer evenings is the productive routine.

## The Dnieper and Sozh around Gomel — southern big-river fishing

Belarus owns a substantial stretch of the upper Dnieper, and near Gomel its confluence partner, the Sozh, delivers classic big-river sport: zander on the channel edges, asp boiling under baitfish in August, catfish in the deep holes below bridge pilings. Sandy beaches make comfortable bank camps, and the gradient is so gentle that one stretch fishes well in almost any wind.

## Paid waters and fish-farm lakes — the easy day out

Belarus has a dense network of platnaya rybalka — pay waters — many run on old fish-farm complexes where carp, crucian and grass carp grow heavy. Eco-parks such as Akva Ptich on the Ptich river southwest of Minsk combine stocked ponds with cottages and saunas, while fishing houses (each region has its Dom Rybaka) rent boats and bunks on the best natural lakes. For families or anyone short on time, these venues guarantee bent rods at modest cost.

## Lake Osveya and the far-northern wilds

Hard against the Latvian border, Lake Osveya is the country's second-largest lake, shallow, fertile and famously mosquito-rich in June. Its island — one of the largest lake islands in Europe — shelters bays where tench and big crucian feed among the lilies, and winter zander fishing through the ice can be exceptional. Few foreign anglers ever see it, which is an argument in itself.

## Practical notes — rules, seasons and access

Fishing with one rod in most public waters is free for residents and visitors alike, but rented and protected waters require a putyovka (day permit) bought locally, and spring closures protect spawning — pike typically in March-April, zander into May; check current dates. The wels season peaks June to September; pike and perch turn on in autumn; ice fishing runs December to March in a normal winter. A visa-free regime covers visitors arriving via Minsk airport for stays up to 30 days (verify current conditions before travel), and Grodno and Brest have had special visa-free tourist zones. Spinning gear, a box of jig heads and warm clothes cover ninety percent of situations.
