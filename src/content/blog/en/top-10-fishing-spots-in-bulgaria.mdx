Bulgaria is a compact country with a disproportionately rewarding fishing geography. The Danube River forms the entire northern border with Romania, stretching more than 470 kilometres and delivering some of the largest wels catfish in Europe. In the interior, the Iskar, Arda, and Struma rivers drain the western and central Rhodope Mountains, producing wild brown trout and grayling in cold, clean water. To the east, the Black Sea coast extends 378 kilometres from Durankulak in the north to Rezovo in the south, with sandy beaches, rocky headlands, and the occasional reef producing turbot, bluefish (lüfer), mullet, and sea bass. Bulgaria's fishing regulations have been progressively tightened since its EU accession in 2007, and the results in many waters are now visible in recovering populations of both trout and predatory species.

## Danube River — Wels Catfish of Legendary Size

The Bulgarian stretch of the Danube, running from Vidin in the northwest to Silistra in the northeast, holds wels catfish (Silurus glanis) that rank among the largest freshwater fish in the world. Fish exceeding 2 metres in length and weighing over 100 kg are caught here every season, with the stretch near Nikopol and Oryahovo being particularly noted. The standard approach involves large livebait — carp, bream, or chub — presented on bottom rigs in the deep channel during night sessions. The Гьола (Gyola) area near the river represents a classic ox-bow lake environment where catfish and carp congregate as the river recedes in summer. Local fishing associations manage some sections and issue day tickets.

## Iskar River — Trout and Grayling in the Balkan Mountains

The Iskar River, Bulgaria's longest entirely domestic river at 368 kilometres, rises near Sofia and passes through the Iskar Gorge — a dramatic canyon north of the capital. The upper Iskar and its tributaries in the Rhodope and Balkan mountains hold wild brown trout and European grayling in fast, cold, well-oxygenated water. The sections above the Iskar Reservoir near Samokov are managed as designated trout zones with catch-and-release requirements during certain periods. The grayling fishing in the Iskar Gorge, particularly between the villages of Lyutibrod and Cherepish, is among the best in the Balkans. Fly fishing with nymphs and wet flies in the autumn produces the most consistent results.

## Язовир Реселец (Resetlets Reservoir)

The Resetlets Reservoir in Pleven Province, created on the Vit River, is one of Bulgaria's most productive artificial lakes for carp and predatory fishing. Язовир Реселец holds large mirror carp and common carp in the 5–15 kg range, as well as pike-perch (zander), perch, and resident wels catfish. The reservoir is accessible from the town of Pleven and has established fishing platforms and camping areas managed by local clubs. The zander fishing here has developed a strong following among sport anglers, with jigging and live-bait techniques on the deeper sections producing fish up to 8 kg.

## Платен Риболов (Pay Fishing Lakes)

The платен риболов (paid fishing) system has developed extensively across Bulgaria, with hundreds of private lakes offering stocked carp, catfish, and trout fishing on a day-permit basis. Спортен риболов Хераково (Sport Fishing Herakovo) near Sofia and similar facilities across the country provide accessible fishing for urban anglers without the need for a full national licence. These stocked lakes hold carp up to 20 kg and maintain water quality through regular management. The concept has expanded to include предварително зарибено езеро (pre-stocked lake) operations in the Rhodopes and Sredna Gora mountains that offer trout fishing for visiting anglers.

## Black Sea Coast — Turbot and Bluefish

The Bulgarian Black Sea coast offers some of the most accessible saltwater fishing in Eastern Europe. Turbot (Psetta maxima) are caught from beach and boat in autumn and spring when they feed actively in shallow water, with the beaches between Burgas and Sozopol particularly noted. Bluefish (Pomatomus saltatrix) — the same species as the Australian tailor — run along the coast from August to October in schools that can be encountered from rocky promontories, piers, and beach casting. The piers at Nesebar, Nessebar Old Town, and Varna's port area attract both local anglers and visitors targeting bluefish, garfish, and mullet year-round. The section of coast near Ropotamo Nature Reserve retains some of the cleanest water on the Bulgarian Black Sea shoreline.

## Ribolovni Kompleks Aytite — Managed Fishing Park

The Риболовен комплекс Айтите (Fishing Complex Aytite) near Gabrovo in the Balkan Mountains is an example of the better-managed private fishing operations in Bulgaria. The complex encompasses several lakes and a section of river stocked with brown trout and rainbow trout, alongside a carp pond with fish averaging 3–8 kg. Accommodation and day passes are available, making it a viable multi-day destination for anglers visiting central Bulgaria. The mountain setting — at around 600 metres in the forested Balkan foothills — adds considerable appeal to what might otherwise be a straightforward stocked fishery.

## Arda River — Wild Trout in the Eastern Rhodopes

The Arda River, flowing from the high eastern Rhodope Mountains through a series of dams and gorges before entering Turkey, holds one of Bulgaria's better wild brown trout populations in its upper reaches near Smolyan and Ardino. The river is cold year-round due to releases from the Kardzhali and Studen Kladenets reservoirs, which stabilise water temperature and clarity even in summer. Spin fishing with small Mepps-style lures and nymphing are both effective. The gorges of the Arda above Madzharovo are particularly scenic, and the region hosts one of the last breeding populations of Egyptian vultures in Europe.

## База за Отдих и Риболов Ягнево (Yagnevo Fishing Base)

The База за отдих и риболов Ягнево (Yagnevo Rest and Fishing Base) in central Bulgaria represents the traditional Bulgarian holiday-fishing model: a riverside lodge with boat hire, camping, and day fishing on a private stretch of river or lake. Operations of this type are scattered along the major rivers — the Yantra, the Maritsa, and the Tundzha — and provide an informal, affordable entry point to Bulgaria's freshwater fishing for visiting anglers. The typical target species are catfish, carp, and asp (Leuciscus aspius), the latter being one of the most sporting surface-feeding coarse fish in European rivers.

## Zlatni Pyasatsi — Coast and Port Fishing Near Varna

The Zlatni Pyasatsi (Golden Sands) resort area north of Varna, though primarily known as a beach holiday destination, sits adjacent to productive coastal fishing waters. The rocky headlands that interrupt the sandy beaches hold sea bass, mullet, and bleak on light tackle. From the breakwaters of the port of Varna — Bulgaria's largest Black Sea port — anglers target bluefish and garfish from September to November. The Varna Lake, a coastal lagoon connected to the sea, produces bream, perch, and catfish from its reed-fringed margins.

## Рибарник (Fish Farm) Lakes and Reservoirs

Bulgaria has dozens of рибарник (fish farm) operations that have diversified into sport fishing. Many fish farms throughout Vidin, Vratsa, Montana, and Pleven provinces allow day fishing in their commercial ponds, which hold carp, tench, and roach at relatively high densities compared to natural waters. While these are purely put-and-take operations, they provide reliable results for anglers who simply want a fish on the bank rather than an expedition into the wilderness.
