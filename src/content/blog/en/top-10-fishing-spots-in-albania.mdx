Albania sits at a crossroads between the Balkans and the Mediterranean, and that geographic luck plays out brilliantly for anglers. Snow-fed rivers carve through limestone gorges in the north, ancient glacial lakes straddle the border to the east, and 476 kilometres of Adriatic and Ionian coastline stretch southward into some of the most underfished salt water in Europe. The fishing pressure here is a fraction of what you'd find in Western Europe, and the landscapes are startling in their severity and beauty.

## Valbona River — Wild Brown Trout in the Albanian Alps

The Valbona rises in the Bjeshkët e Namuna range at roughly 2,600 metres and tumbles south through a valley that UNESCO recognises as one of the most scenically important in the Balkans. Its upper reaches between Valbona village and Rragam are prime dry-fly water. Brown trout here run to 45 centimetres in the deeper pools, feeding heavily on mayflies from late April through June. The riverbed is mostly cobble and limestone shelves; wading is manageable in summer but flows spike sharply after afternoon thunderstorms. The Valbona National Park authorities require a daily fishing permit, available at the Bajram Curri forestry office, costing around 500 Albanian lek.

## Shala River — Turquoise Gorge Fishing

The Shala cuts through vertical limestone cliffs north of Shkodër, and its colour — a vivid turquoise-green from dissolved minerals — makes it look more like a Caribbean lagoon than a mountain river. Grayling share the water with brown trout in the lower sections, and the gradient eases into long glassy runs ideal for nymph fishing. Access is largely by boat from Koman Lake; the gorge road is unsealed and demanding in wet conditions. The best fishing window is May through September, with water temperatures optimal for surface activity in June and July.

## Lake Ohrid (Albanian Shore) — Endemic Ohrid Trout

Lake Ohrid is one of the oldest lakes in the world, estimated at 3 to 5 million years old, and home to roughly 200 endemic species. The jewel for anglers is the Ohrid trout (*Salmo letnica*), a deep-bodied lake fish that once supported a significant commercial fishery before regulations tightened. The Albanian shoreline between Lin and Pogradec offers boat fishing in water down to 30 metres, where trout in the 1.5 to 2 kilogram range patrol the thermocline. Fishing is permitted under strict seasonal rules — the closed season runs from November to February — and a licence from the Albanian Ministry of Environment is mandatory.

## Shkumbin River — Coarse Fishing and Cyprinids

The Shkumbin divides Albania roughly in two as it runs east to west from the Elbasan highlands to the Adriatic. In its middle reaches near Librazhd, the river holds healthy populations of chub, barbel, and nase. These species are underrated sporting fish in the Balkans; chub in the Shkumbin regularly top 1.5 kilograms and take streamer flies aggressively in the summer heat when trout retreat to deeper, cooler water. The river also marks the approximate boundary between the Gheg and Tosk Albanian dialect regions, so local knowledge here can be challenging to gather — bring a phrase book.

## Prespa Lakes — Remote Carp and Eel

Little Prespa and Great Prespa straddle the tri-border junction of Albania, North Macedonia, and Greece. Albanian access is via the village of Liqenas on the shore of Little Prespa. The lakes hold large common carp to over 10 kilograms, plus substantial eel populations that migrate down the Devoll drainage. Coarse anglers fishing the reed margins after dark with boilies or worm account for most of the carp. The area is a designated biosphere reserve, and catching eels for the table is now banned; barbless hooks and careful handling are expected.

## Ionian Coast — Sea Fishing for Dentex and Sea Bream

The Riviera Albaneze between Sazan Island and the Greek border at Butrint is the Ionian coastline at its most dramatic — steep cliffs, hidden coves, and water visibility exceeding 20 metres on calm days. Rocky-bottom boat fishing targets dentex (*Dentex dentex*), a powerful predator that grows to 6 kilograms or more around the submerged reefs off Cape Gjuhëza. Gilt-head sea bream, two-banded bream, and the occasional European bass round out the catch. Charter boats operate out of Sarandë and Himarë; the best months are June through October when water temperatures reach 22–24°C.

## Adriatic Coast — Surf Casting and Mullet

The northern Adriatic shore between Shëngjin and Vlorë is shallower and sandier than the Ionian, with lagoon systems behind the barrier beaches holding thick-lipped mullet year-round. Surf casting from Divjakë or the beaches near Rodon Cape produces sea bass at night during summer, while daytime fishing with float tackle and bread targets mullet to 2 kilograms. The Divjakë-Karavasta National Park lagoon is a protected area where fishing from boats requires a special permit, but shore fishing outside designated sanctuary zones is permitted.

## Drini River — Large River Fishing near Shkodër

The Drini (or Black Drin) enters Lake Shkodër from the southeast, carrying cold clear water from its upper tributaries in North Macedonia. In the lower reaches between Koplik and the lake, the river holds pike, perch, and the occasional Huchen (*Hucho hucho*) — the Danube salmon — which are rare and must be returned. Winter spinning with large lures accounts for pike over 8 kilograms in the slower backwaters. A boat is helpful here; the banks are often overgrown with willows and reed.

## Lake Shkodër — Freshwater Bream and Carp

Lake Shkodër is the largest lake in the Balkans, shared with Montenegro, covering 370 square kilometres. Its Albanian shore is accessible near Shiroka, 8 kilometres north of Shkodër city. The lake holds enormous shoals of freshwater bream, white bream, and roach, plus carp to double figures. Ground-bait approach from the shore is most productive in spring when bream mass in the shallows. Eels are also abundant and actively targeted by local anglers using nightlines, though sport anglers generally prefer the surface-feeding action on summer evenings.
