Kosovo may be Europe's youngest and smallest country, but its rivers punch well above their weight for wild trout fishing. The country straddles the Dinaric and Šar Mountain ranges, and the headwaters of both the Drin and Ibar river systems rise here in cold karstic springs. Much of the fishing country is accessed via rough mountain roads or on foot, and the crowds that define southern European fishing are entirely absent. This is Balkan trout fishing at its most authentic.

## Ibar River — Rugova Gorge Brown Trout

The Ibar rises just south of Peć (Peja) in the glacially carved Rugova Gorge, one of the longest and deepest gorges in the Balkans at nearly 25 kilometres long and up to 1,000 metres deep. The river here is narrow, fast, and intensely oxygenated — ideal for wild brown trout that average 35 centimetres, with fish to 55 centimetres taken from the larger plunge pools. The Rugova Gorge road runs along the river from Peć to the ski resort of Ski Centar Brezovica, offering access points at every curve. Fly fishing with weighted bead-head nymphs dominates; a short upstream nymph cast into the white water behind boulders produces the best results. The permit system operates through the Kosovo Federation of Sport Fishing, with annual licences valid throughout Kosovo.

## White Drin — Chalk Stream Fishing

The White Drin (Drini i Bardhë) drains much of western Kosovo westward toward Albania. In its upper reaches near Deçan and Junik, the river is fed by karstic springs that maintain temperatures of 9 to 11°C year-round, producing chalk-stream conditions more reminiscent of an English chalk river than a Balkan torrent. The riverbed of cream-coloured limestone is easily visible through the gin-clear water, and brown trout hold in classic chalk-stream lies behind weed beds and in the lip of undercut banks. Fishing from late April through June with CDC dry flies is exceptional. The Deçan monastery area sees occasional visitors, and local guides from Gjakova can arrange access.

## Bistrica e Pejës — Mountain Tributary Fishing

The Bistrica e Pejës is a major left-bank tributary of the White Drin, dropping from the Prokletije Mountains (the Albanian Alps) at over 2,400 metres to the Peć valley in a series of waterfalls and steep boulder-strewn rapids. The section between the village of Lloqan and the confluence is fast, shallow, and full of small but spirited brown trout averaging 20–28 centimetres. This water is best suited to high-stick nymphing — a technique where a long leader fishes a weighted nymph directly below the rod tip with little or no fly line on the water.

## Mirusha Waterfalls Area — Gorge Fishing

The Mirusha River in central Kosovo is best known for its sequence of 17 waterfalls and pools in a narrow limestone gorge near Klina. The pools between the waterfalls hold brown trout in cool, clear water even in the height of summer when surrounding streams run warm and low. Access to the gorge is via the Mirusha Waterfalls Nature Park; a modest entrance fee applies. The pools are small enough to require delicate presentation; dry-fly fishing with size 14–16 patterns imitating the abundant midges and stoneflies produces fish reliably.

## Liqeni i Badovcit — Coarse Fishing near Pristina

For anglers based in the capital Pristina, the Badovc Reservoir 12 kilometres east of the city provides accessible coarse fishing. The reservoir holds large carp, pike-perch, and catfish. Carp to 8 kilograms are taken regularly by ground-bait fishing from the eastern bank, where the water depth reaches 6 metres. This is a good option in midsummer when the mountain rivers are low and warm. Fishing permits are sold at the reservoir car park.

## Šar Planina Lakes — High Alpine Fishing

The Šar Planina range forms Kosovo's southern border with North Macedonia and contains dozens of glacial lakes above 2,000 metres. The most accessible cluster is above Brezovica ski resort; Lake Livadičko Jezero at 2,100 metres holds wild brown trout in 1 to 2 kilogram range. The hike from the ski resort takes around 90 minutes on a well-marked path. These lakes see minimal fishing pressure; the trout are wary but will take a dry fly in the evening when midges swarm over the surface. No permit is required above 2,000 metres in the Šar Planina section.

## Fish Farm PESHK ISTOGU — Stocked Waters for Beginners

Peshk Istogu is a well-maintained fish farm and put-and-take fishery near Istog in western Kosovo, offering rainbow trout fishing in managed ponds fed by spring water. It is a useful introduction to fly fishing for newcomers and a reliable option when natural rivers are in flood after spring snowmelt. Rainbow trout in the 0.5 to 1.5 kilogram range are stocked regularly; fly fishing and spinning are both permitted. The facility includes basic accommodation.

## Drini i Zi — Black Drin Upstream Reaches

The upper Black Drin (Drini i Zi) in the Kukës area near the Albanian border runs through gorge scenery and holds a population of brown trout in clear, fast water. This area is somewhat difficult to access — a four-wheel-drive is recommended for the last 12 kilometres from the tarmac road. The fish are smaller than in the White Drin but extremely wild; the landscape, with the North Albanian Alps visible to the west, is unforgettable. Early season fishing from March to May is best here.

## Lepenac River — Headwaters above Kaçanik Gorge

The Lepenac is Kosovo's easternmost significant river, rising near Štimlje and flowing south through the dramatic Kaçanik Gorge into North Macedonia. In its upper sections above the gorge, the river holds small but plentiful brown trout in a narrow valley flanked by beech woods. The gorge itself is a civil engineering spectacle — a railway line and the E75 motorway squeeze through the narrows together — but upstream of the gorge the river is wild and quiet.
