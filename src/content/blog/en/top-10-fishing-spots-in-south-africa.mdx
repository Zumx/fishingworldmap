Few countries pack as much fishing into one coastline and one interior as South Africa. The cold Benguela current collides with the warm Agulhas off the Cape, creating tuna grounds of world rank; the Wild Coast serves up surf-casting that borders on the mythical; and inland, the Vaal and Orange rivers hold smallmouth yellowfish — a freckled, hard-running native that fly anglers cross hemispheres to catch. Add highland trout, Zululand estuaries and dams stuffed with carp and bass, and a single trip barely scratches it. These ten spots show the spread.

## The Wild Coast — surf-casting at its rawest

Between Port St Johns and Coffee Bay, the Transkei's Wild Coast remains one of the great shore-fishing wildernesses: green hills dropping to empty points, ragged ledges and river mouths like the Mbotyi, Mtentu and Mzimvubu. Anglers cast for kob (mulloway), garrick (leervis), bronze bream and the brutal musselcracker, while winter brings shad runs and, with them, the predators. During the famous sardine run of June and July, the entire food chain pushes within casting range. Access often means gravel roads and rondavel guesthouses — part of the charm.

## Cape Point tuna grounds — yellowfin within day range

Out of Hout Bay and Simon's Town, sport boats run 20 to 40 nautical miles to the canyons and temperature breaks where the Benguela meets warmer water. From roughly October to June, yellowfin tuna average 40-60 kg with genuine 100 kg fish every season, plus longfin tuna in the cooler months. Stand-up fights on 80 lb gear with Table Mountain on the horizon are the signature image of Cape fishing. Weather windows are everything; build slack days into any trip.

## The Vaal River — smallmouth yellowfish on the fly

The Vaal between Parys and the Vredefort Dome is the spiritual home of yellowfishing. Smallmouth yellowfish of 1-3 kg feed in fast, rocky riffles and take nymphs with a power that shocks visiting trout anglers; largemouth yellowfish, the rarer giant of the system, can exceed 10 kg. Wading the warm rapids in summer (October to April) with a strike indicator is the classic method. Stretches such as Vaal River Angling near the barrage and the riverside camps around Parys make access easy.

## Sterkfontein Dam — sight-fishing paradise

High in the Drakensberg foothills near Harrismith, Sterkfontein Dam holds gin-clear water and dense shoals of smallmouth yellowfish that cruise the banks sipping terrestrials. On calm summer days anglers sight-cast dry flies — hoppers and beetles — to fish they can watch eat. It is regularly compared to New Zealand flats fishing, at a fraction of the cost. Strictly catch-and-release, and all the better for it.

## Dullstroom and the Mpumalanga highlands

The trout triangle around Dullstroom, Machadodorp and Waterval Boven sits at 2,000 m, with frosty mornings and stillwaters that grow rainbow trout beyond 4 kg. Dozens of syndicate and day-ticket waters dot the plateau, and the nearby Elands River offers wild river fish. Winter (May to August) is prime, when cold water keeps trout active all day. Country hotels with fireplaces and whisky bars complete a very particular South African highlands ritual.

## Sodwana Bay and the Zululand coast — billfish off the beach

Sodwana, inside the iSimangaliso Wetland Park, is famous for launching ski-boats through the surf — a white-knuckle ride that ends in marlin water within a few kilometres. Black, blue and striped marlin run November to April; sailfish, wahoo, king mackerel and giant trevally fill the rest of the calendar. Further south, the St Lucia and Richards Bay areas offer estuary fishing for grunter and kob where the park's zonation allows it.

## The Orange River — yellowfish in desert canyons

Along the Northern Cape border, the Orange cuts through desert rock past Augrabies Falls, where the river thunders 56 m into a granite gorge. Multi-day raft-and-fish trips drift anglers through canyons that see almost no pressure, catching smallmouth and largemouth yellowfish plus hard-pulling mudfish. Nights are spent on sandbanks under Karoo stars. The combination of solitude, scenery and fish numbers is unmatched in the country.

## The Breede River and Cape estuaries

The Breede, entering the sea at Witsand, is legendary for outsized kob — fish over 50 kg have been recorded in its deep holes — plus grunter, garrick and spotted grunter tailing on the flats. Strict size and bag limits now protect the kob fishery, and most serious anglers release everything. Nearby, the Goukou at Stilbaai and the Knysna lagoon offer gentler estuary sessions among milkwood trees and houseboats.

## KwaZulu-Natal midlands and battlefield dams

Around Vryheid, Utrecht and Dundee, a quieter fishing culture thrives on farm dams and club waters — bass, carp and barbel handled by local outfits such as the Utrecht Angling Club, with trout in the higher streams toward Wakkerstroom. In the Southern Drakensberg, the Giant's Cup Wilderness Reserve area near Underberg holds brown trout streams that fish beautifully in autumn. It is unglamorous, friendly fishing — braais, bakkies and big skies.

## Gauteng's urban escape valves

For the Johannesburg and Pretoria millions, fishing means after-work sessions at venues like Wolwespruit Nature Reserve on the Rietvlei system, the trout dams of Eendekuil Flyfishing toward the Vaal catchment, and bass venues like Rust de Winter and Bronkhorstspruit dams. Carp fishing — both classic boilie work and the local mielie-bomb style — is a genuine national subculture, and weekend competitions on Hartbeespoort and Vaal dams draw hundreds of rods.

## Practical notes — seasons, permits and safety

Coastal fishing requires a recreational permit sold at post offices; freshwater rules vary by province, and trout and yellowfish waters are mostly privately accessed via day tickets. Summer (November to March) suits the Vaal, Sterkfontein and billfish; winter favours trout, Cape longfin tuna and the sardine run. Distances are large — Cape Town to Sodwana is over 1,700 km — so most visitors pick one or two regions per trip. A 4x4 is useful on the Wild Coast and essential for Sodwana beach launches.
