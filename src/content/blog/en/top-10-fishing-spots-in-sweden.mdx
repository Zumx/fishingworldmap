Few countries reward a travelling angler like Sweden. The coastline shatters into roughly 30,000 islands in the Stockholm archipelago alone, the interior holds nearly 100,000 lakes, and above the Arctic Circle the June sun simply refuses to set, so the evening rise lasts all night. Add the right of public access that lets you roam freely, a fishing-permit system that is cheap and digital, and fish ranging from metre-long pike to sea-run brown trout of 10 kg, and you have one of Europe's great fishing nations. These ten waters show its full range.

## The Stockholm archipelago

The brackish Baltic around Stockholm is one of the world's premier pike fisheries. Salinity is so low that pike, perch and zander thrive among the skerries, and fish over a metre are caught every week of the season. Spring and late autumn are prime, when big females stack up in shallow bays ahead of spawning. Guides run fast skiffs out of Stockholm, Nynäshamn and Vaxholm, casting big soft plastics and jerkbaits along reed edges. Best of all, fishing with handheld gear is free along the entire Baltic coast — no permit needed.

## Mörrumsån, Blekinge

The Mörrum is Sweden's most storied river. Kronolaxfisket in Mörrum village has managed the fishery since 1941, and the season premiere on the last weekend of March is a national event, with anglers drawing beats in the frosty dawn. Sea trout here are genuinely huge — fish over 10 kg are landed most years — and the spring salmon run brings chrome fish well past 15 kg into pools like Kungsforsen. Fly water is classic and demanding, with strong currents and deep glides. Book beats early; the famous pools sell out months ahead.

## Kaitum and the Lapland grayling rivers

North of the Arctic Circle, the Kaitum, Torne and Lainio river systems hold grayling of a size most Europeans only dream about — fish of 50 cm and beyond sip dry flies under the midnight sun. The season runs roughly mid-June to early September, and in high summer you can fish around the clock. Helicopter-access camps such as Miekak in the Arjeplog mountains add wild Arctic char to the menu. Mosquitoes are ferocious in July, so bring a head net and fish the breezy gravel bars.

## Lake Vänern and the Gullspång salmon

Vänern, at 5,650 square kilometres the largest lake in the EU, holds landlocked Atlantic salmon and trout of the famous Gullspång strain, which grow past 15 kg on a diet of vendace. Trolling is the main method, with spring and late autumn the peak windows out of harbours like Mariestad and Kristinehamn. The lake also offers superb pike and zander fishing in the Lurö archipelago. Vänern is big water that demands respect — sudden winds build steep waves — but a guided day here can produce the salmon of a lifetime without ever seeing the sea.

## Gotland's sea trout coast

The limestone island of Gotland may offer Europe's best shore fishing for sea trout. From October to April, wading anglers cover rocky flats and current seams with spinners and streamer flies, and catches of several fish over 60 cm in a session are realistic. The east coast around Östergarn and the southern point at Hoburgen are classic stretches. Coastal fishing is free, but note the closed season rules on the north coast and the protected zones near river mouths during autumn spawning — local tackle shops in Visby keep up-to-date maps.

## Byskeälven and the Baltic salmon rivers

The wild salmon rivers of Västerbotten and Norrbotten — Byskeälven, Åbyälven and the mighty Kalixälven — have rebounded spectacularly, with tens of thousands of salmon now climbing them each summer. The Byske is the most accessible, a forest river with well-marked pools, camping platforms and a generous permit system. Peak migration runs from mid-June through July, and fish between 8 and 15 kg are common. Spin and fly anglers share most beats, and the long northern daylight means the best fishing often happens close to midnight.

## Åsnen and the Småland lake district

Southern Sweden's lake plateau is pike and zander country. Lake Åsnen, partly a national park, is a shallow, island-strewn labyrinth where pike fishing stays strong all season, and nearby Bolmen and Möckeln fish similarly. The region is also dotted with managed put-and-take waters — Butorp Ädelfiske stocks rainbow trout in forest ponds, and family-friendly sites such as Snogeholm Fritid down in Skåne combine easy fishing with canoe rental and cabins. It is the ideal region for a first fishing holiday with children, with boat ramps and campsites everywhere.

## Vättern's Arctic char and the deep-water specialists

Crystal-clear Lake Vättern is a strange and wonderful fishery: an ice-age relic where Arctic char patrol depths of 30 metres and more, alongside huge brown trout and a remnant salmon stocking program. Locals troll with downriggers or jig vertically over deep humps, and the char average a kilo with fish over three caught annually. Winter ice fishing for char off Visingsö island is a beloved tradition when conditions allow. The water is so clear that fluorocarbon leaders and small spoons make a measurable difference.

## Permits, seasons and the practical bits

Sweden makes fishing logistics easy. The five big lakes — Vänern, Vättern, Hjälmaren, Mälaren and Storsjön — plus the entire Baltic coast are free for rod-and-line fishing. Everywhere else you buy a fiskekort, the local fishing permit, usually online in two minutes for 10 to 20 euros a day. Northern mountain waters use the statens kort system for vast wilderness areas. Seasons matter: pike fishing peaks April-May and October-November, salmon June-August, sea trout on the coasts October-April, and grayling June-September. Check local rules — minimum sizes and protected spawning areas vary water by water.
